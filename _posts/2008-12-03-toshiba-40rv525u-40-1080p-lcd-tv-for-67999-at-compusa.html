Toshiba's 40RV525U is a 40" LCD TV sporting a pixel format of 1920 x 1080. That means 1080p without any scaling (scaling degrades picture quality). the 40RV525U has a Cinespeed LCD panel and that just means that the response time is 8ms or less. If it is 8ms then it's on the slow side, but if it is considerably less, say 4ms, then it would be on the fast side. I believe Toshiba created the name Cinespeed so it can procure a variety of panels that could fit the <8ms specification. The 40" Toshiba LCD TV has the requisite ATSC tuner so you can enjoy HDTV and it has three HDMI inputs. The 40RV525U does not have any motion blur reduction technologies. For those of you who live and die watching sports, this might not be your TV. But for those who must have a 40" TV for a great price, look no further: CompUSA has it for an incredible $679.99!
