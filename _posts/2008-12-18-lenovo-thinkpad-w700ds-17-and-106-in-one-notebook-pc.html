Model: E7M

Aspect Ratio: 16:10 (17"), 10:16 (10.6")
Pixel Format: 1920 x 1200 (17"), 768 x 1280 (10.6")
Webcam: 1.3MP

HDD: 320GB (x2 160GB 7200RPM)
Optical: DVD Writer
Connectivity: ExpressCard (54 and 34), WiFi (AGN), Gigabit Ethernet, FireWire, Mic-In, Headphone-Out, USB 2.0 (5), Bluetooth
Other: Fingerprint Reader, Color Calibration, Digitizer and Pen
OS: Windows Vista Business (64-bit)
Battery: 9-cell Lithium Ion

Lenovo has come up with something quite unique. The ThinkPad W700 was unique enough with a built in Wacom digitizer and integrated color calibration. But the W700ds version is even more different. The W700ds has two screens. One is your ordinary 17" TFT LCD with a CCFL backlight sporting a 1920 x 1200 pixel format. The second display is a 10.2" TFT LCD arranged in portrait mode. So the pixel format is 768 x 1280. The 10.2" also has a LED backlight, which was probably necessary since it will have to be very thin to fold back in somewhere. I would be very interested in knowing how the 10.2" LCD slides or folds back into the main unit. the extra screen real estate is welcome but I think it adds a bit too much in terms of complexity, weight, cost, etc. I would much rather have a 17" LCD with a 2560 x 1600 pixel format. The regular single LCD W700 is a whopping $2586.99 at Amazon.com. I wonder how much more this W700ds beast is going to cost; probably north of $3000!
