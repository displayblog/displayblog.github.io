Nokia E71x Specifications

Display: 2.4" TFT LCD
Pixel Format: 320 x 240
Camera: 3.2MP with Auto Focus and Flash
Storage: 120MB Internal, microSD (up to 8GB)
Network: GSM/GPRS/EDGE (850/900/1800/1900MHz), UMTS/HSDPA (850/1900MHz)
Connectivity: GPS, WiFi BG, MicroUSB, Bluetooth 2.0 + EDR & A2DP, Infrared
OS: Symbian S60 3.2
Battery: 1500mAh Lithium Ion (4.5 hours talk, 12 days standby)
Dimensions: 4.4 x 2.2 x 0.4"
Weight: 4.4oz
Availability: Around May 2009 on AT&T

Nokia unveiled its E71x smartphone at the CTIA Wireless 2009 tradeshow that is being held in Las Vegas. The E71x is an evolved E71. The E71x will cost just $99 with a 2-year contract with AT&T--much more affordable than the unlocked E71 you can purchase for a bit more than $300. But you do get your unlocked freedom for the extra $200--necessary if you travel and don't want to make AT&T rich.

What's to like? The keyboard seems to be getting a lot of positive feedback. The 3.2MP camera takes decent pictures and it has auto focus and a flash. You can have a video call, which is nifty and will probably require a good data connection. The 2.4" TFT LCD looks to be a fine display with a 320 x 240 pixel format. I would rather have more screen than buttons but there are quite a few who would rather have the buttons.

What's not to like? It's locked to AT&T.
