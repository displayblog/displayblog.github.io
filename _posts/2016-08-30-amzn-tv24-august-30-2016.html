Click here for a brief description about DISPLAYBLOG's AMZN.TV24 market research. Today I'll be answering the questions: What are the most popular sizes for 4K? And 1080p?

1080p Size Share


There are five sizes that make up almost all of the 1080p TVs that show up on the front search result page on Amazon. Here are some observations:

  • 55-inch TVs have slowly grown over the last 12 data points and now have captured 40%.
  • 50-inch TVs were the largest just 7 days ago, but now has dropped down to 20% for a tie for #2.
  • 60-inch TVs are tied for #2.
  • 65-inch and 75-inch 1080p TVs make up 10% each.


As I've mentioned before 55-inch is becoming the minimum size for a living room TV, and it might also become the minimum size for most TVs: family room, bedrooms, offices, boardrooms, meeting rooms, mancaves, etc.

I think 50-inch will continue to dwindle as we move to larger TVs. For 2016 55-inch 1080p TVs will be the most popular, but soon after that 60-inch will be at 1080p.

4K Size Share


Let's start off with some observations:

  • 55-inch dominates with 40%+ share.
  • 65-inch is at #2 with roughly 30% share.
  • 60-inch is at #3 followed by 70-inch.
  • 50-inch has fallen out.


Although 20%+ of 1080p TVs are 50 inch, there are none at 4K. The reason I think is because consumers want something bigger when it comes to upgrading to 4K. A 4K TV is a major upgrade to most consumers since it requires additional spending to acquire 4K content.

Similar to 1080p, 55-inch TVs are dominant and will probably continue to be at #1 throughout 2016.

Unlike 1080p, 65-inch is #2 with about 30% share. It would seem 60-inch should be #2, but for some reason TV brands and LCD manufacturers have decided 65-inch is the better size for 4K. So look for 2017 to be when 65-inch, not 60-inch, becomes the most popular size.
