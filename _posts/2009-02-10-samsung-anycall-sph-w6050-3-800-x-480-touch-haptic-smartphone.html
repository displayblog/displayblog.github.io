Samsung's SPH-W6050 is a high-end smartphone sporting a 3" TFT LCD with touch and haptic feedback. The 3" LCD features a 800 x 480 (landscape) pixel format and looks pretty nice. On the back is a huge lens coupled with a 5MP CMOS image sensor. The picture quality is very good but there is some shutter lag so you'll need to hold steady. Yes, even with the anti-shake technology fast moving objects will probably be quite blurry. The DMB functionality is quite cool: you can watch pretty much wherever you are in Korea. The SPH-W6050 is a bit smaller than an iPhone 3G but is a bit thicker. At first I couldn't figure out how to turn the SPH-W6050 on: there is a rubber "Hold" button the right side of the device. You have to press that button and then press it again to unlock. Notice also the front-facing camera: that's for video communications and the SPH-W6050 will let you do that for 2 hours.
