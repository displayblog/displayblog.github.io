via The Verge. The Kindle Fire HD and iPad mini both sport IPS LCDs. The Kindle Fire HD has a pixel format of 1280x800; the iPad mini has 1024x768. Both are not ideal for HD viewing—1280x720 is—but the iPad mini is worse, because it can't technically. Both fall short of being retina displays, but the 163-ppi resolution of the iPad mini is pitiful and comes in at less than the 165-ppi iPhone, iPhone 3G, and iPhone 3GS.
