HP Pavilion tx2500z Specifications
Display: 12.1" Touch TFT LCD
Aspect Ratio: 16:10
Pixel Format: 1280 x 800
GPU: ATI Radeon HD 3200
CPU: AMD Turion X2 Ultra ZM (Puma)
RAM: Up to 4GB DDR2
Storage: 320GB
Optical: DVD Burner
Connectivity: WiFi (802.11a/b/g/n), Bluetooth (optional), 1000 Ethernet, 56K Modem, USB 2.0 (3), ExpressCard
Other: Fingerprint reader, Webcam, 5-in-1 Media Card Reader
OS: Windows Vista
Weight: 4.23lbs
Price: US$1049

I like the underdog to win, from time to time. In the case of the CPU market, the underdog is AMD. AMD's dual core Puma CPU is powering HP's tx2500z. Not sure that means a whole lot since tablet PCs make up a tiny slice of the overall notebook PC market, but I'm glad to see a big guy like HP continue to work with AMD. The 12.1" TFT LCD with a pixel format of 1280 x 800 is pretty standard fare. What would have been great is a multitouch screen (like the iPhone) instead and a HP-optimized UI, like its TouchSmart 2. It can't be that hard to make something like that, right?
