Kodak OLED Wireless Frame Specifications

Display: 7.6" Wide OLED Display
Pixel Format: 800 x 480
Contrast Ratio: 30,000:1
Viewing Angle: 180/180
Internal Storage: 2GB
Speakers: Yes
Connectivity: Flickr, WiFi
Price: MSRP US$999

The world is going toward OLED! Well, not quite. Not yet. Sony started the show last year by bringing us the uber-expensive yet ultra-cool XEL-1 11" OLED TV. Now it's Kodak's turn to keep with the tradition, at least one part of it: uber-expensive. I'm not sure about ultra-cool. Wireless digital photo frame: done that. 800 x 480 pixel format: kinda old. 30,000:1 contrast ratio: In a world of 1,000,000:1 CR, not such a big deal. 180/180 viewing angles: just 2 more degrees than the best. 7.6" in size: unique but not that cool and how about something a little bit bigger? So, what's so great about this OLED Wireless Frame from Kodak? Just that it sports an OLED display and is really thin, except for the fat base. Like the XEL-1 you have to deal with a really porky base. How about a OLED wireless digital photo frame that I can hang on the wall? And $999? You must be kidding. I don't know how much CMEL is charging Kodak, but it seems its a bit too much.
