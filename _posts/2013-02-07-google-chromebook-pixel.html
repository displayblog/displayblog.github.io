Ars Technica: Rumor has it Google is working on a Chromebook Pixel, with a 2560x1700 touch display. That's an unusual 1.5:1 aspect ratio. If Google is working on a Chromebook Pixel it would probably be 2560x1440 (16:9) or 2560x1600 (16:10).

The Gorilla Glass covered touch LCD is 12.85 inches and sports a pixel format of 2560x1700. I originally thought the Chromebook Pixel would feature a 16:9 or 16:10 display; I was wrong. The 2560x1700 pixel format translate to a 3:2 aspect ratio. The same year Apple goes from 3:2 to 16:9 on the iPhone 5, Google decides to go from 16:9 to 3:2 for its top-of-the-line Chromebook effort.

With 2560x1700 you can put 1280x1700 side by side. This would be awesome for the way I work. I agree with Google here: With 1700 vertical pixels we'll be scrolling up and down a lot less. I like the blocky design, too.

Priced at US$1299 or $1449 with LTE, but keep in mind a full blown 13.3-inch 2560x1600 MacBook Pro can be had for $1499. But if modders hack the Chromebook Pixel to run other operating systems the steep price might end up being not so steep.
