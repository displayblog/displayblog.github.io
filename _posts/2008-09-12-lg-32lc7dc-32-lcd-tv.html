LG 32LC7DC Specifications

Display: 31.5" TFT LCD
Aspect Ratio: 16:9
Pixel Format: 1366 x 768
Contrast Ratio: 6000:1 (dynamic)
Tuner: ATSC, NTSC, QAM
Brightness: 500 cd/m2
Response Time: 5ms
Viewing Angle: 178/178
Video Processing: XD Engine
Connectivity: HDMI with HDCP (2), LG SIMPLINK (HDMI-CEC), Composite (1), Component (1), VGA, RS-232C, USB (Service only), S-Video, RF In (1)
Power Consumption: 142W (operational), <1W (standby), Energy Star compliant
Dimensions: 31.75 x 21.75 x 3.11" (w/o stand), 31.75 x 23.80 x 9.80" (w/stand)
Weight: 24.7lbs (w/o stand), 30.2lbs (w/stand)

LG's 32LC7DC is a 32" LCD TV geared for commercial applications such as B2B, healthcare, hospitality and IT. In most commercial applications high-resolution displays are not necessary as most people who will be seeing it are quite a distance away. So in this context the content that is created are generally vivid images with big text. The pixel format, true to the intended marketplace, is just 1366 x 768. Dynamic contrast ratio is a healthy 6000:1, but nothing that will knock your socks off. It has a built-in tuner that supports ATSC, NTSC and QAM. I am not sure, but looking at the very good viewing angles of 178/178, I would guess that it is using an IPS panel. The response time is a fairly fast 5ms. The 32LC7DC is Energy Star rated and sips less than 1W when in standby mode, a feature that is quite important in a commercial application since will almost never be turned off. But just because it is geared toward the commercial market doesn't mean you the regular consumer can't buy it.

Source: LG Commercial
