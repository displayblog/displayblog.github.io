B&H: At $599 the WiFi-only Motorola Xoom is the regular Motorola Xoom minus the 3G for the same price. Of course you aren't tied down to a two-year agreement with the WiFi-only version, so that's a good thing. But I wouldn't spend that kind of money on a WiFi-only tablet. Instead I would save myself $200 and get an iPad 2.
