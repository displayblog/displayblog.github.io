Apple iPhone 3G Specifications
Display: 3.5" (unchanged)
Pixel Format: 480 x 320 (unchanged)
Camera: 2MP (unchanged)
Connectivity: 3G, GPS (A-GPS)
Design: Black back, tapered edges (think MacBook Air)
Color: Black (8GB and 16GB) and white (16GB)
Battery: 300 hours (standby), 5 hours (talk), 5-6 hours (browsing), 7 hours (video), 24 hours (audio)
Price: $199 (8GB), $299 (16GB)

The iPhone 3G is 36% faster than the Nokia N95 and Treo 750. It took just 21 seconds to load a full page on the iPhone 3G. WiFi took 17 seconds while EDGE took 59 seconds. 3G is looking pretty good. The design is all black (plastic) on the back. The prices! They are even cheaper than what I expected!!! $199 for the 8GB? That's great! And just $299 for the 16GB!

Steve mentioned that "the price is a maximum of $199 all around the world..." Subsidized prices at $199 for the iPhone 3G 8GB. What does this mean for the 16GB model? The iPhone 3G 16GB only available at Apple stores? Some very cool features include geotagged photos, batch deletes, flush headphone jack (3.5mm) that will work with regular 3.5mm earphones. The iPhone 3G has ten radios that include:

3x GSM bands
4x 3G bands
Bluetooth
WiFi (802.11b/g no 'n')
and one more of something

Update 2008.06.09 4:50PM PST
Gizmodo has an update that shares some unfortunate news. The iPhone 3G is exceptionally unique, but when it comes to activation and monthly service fees, it's just the same as any other smartphone out there. Let me elaborate.

$30 for unlimited 3G data for consumers
$45 for unlimited 3G data for business users
No in-home activation, you'll need to go to a store: pick either AT&T or the Apple Store
