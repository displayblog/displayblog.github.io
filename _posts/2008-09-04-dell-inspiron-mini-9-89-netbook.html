Webcam: 1.3MP
Color: Alpine White

Of course there are many different in-between configurations to suit your taste. For simple web surfing, email checking and the occasional Office document viewing, the minimum Ubuntu configuration for just $349 sounds pretty good. The 8.9" wide TFT LCD with a pixel format of 1024 x 600 should be just about perfect for web surfing on the side. I'm not sure about the keyboard but if you don't have gigantic hands, you should be able to get by without cramping your fingers. The LED backlight will help save battery power and the absence of Bluetooth will not only save you a few bucks but will also keep the netbook simpler and sipping a bit less battery juice. But then again...

...for just an extra $170 you can get everything (I don't know about the white color, which is a $25 option) and not miss a beat, especially since you'll be getting a 4x increase in storage to 16GB SSD. The integrated 1.3MP webcam is nice just in case you want to video chat with someone on-the-go and so is the Bluetooth connectivity where you can use your Bluetooth headset to chat instead of carrying around a wired headset with mic. You can get the Mini 9 with Windows XP on it, but I'm guessing it'll slow down the netbook a bit.

And finally, you can get the Inpsiron Mini 9 for just $99 with the purchase of a Studio 15, XPS M1530 or XPS M1330, but for a limited time until September 9.
